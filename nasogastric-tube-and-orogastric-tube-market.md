<p><p>Nasogastric (NG) and orogastric (OG) tubes are medical devices used for gastric access. The nasogastric tube is inserted through the nose, passed down the esophagus, and into the stomach. This tube is primarily used for feeding patients who cannot eat orally, for administering medications, or for gastric decompression. Conversely, the orogastric tube is inserted through the mouth and serves similar purposes but is often employed in unconscious or sedated patients where nasal insertion may be contraindicated.</p><p>The Nasogastric Tube and Orogastric Tube Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.20% during the forecast period. Moreover, the aging population and growing healthcare expenditure contribute to the market's expansion. Trends indicate a shift towards disposable and pre-assembled systems that reduce infection risks, along with innovations in materials to improve biocompatibility.
