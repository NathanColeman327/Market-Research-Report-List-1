<p><p>The palm butter market encompasses two main types: crude palm oil and palm olein. Crude palm oil, extracted from the fruit of the oil palm, is unrefined and primarily used for cooking and industrial applications. Palm olein, refined from crude palm oil, is the liquid fraction that remains after fractionation. It is commonly utilized in food products and frying owing to its favorable frying properties. Both variants play crucial roles in global edible oil markets due to their versatility and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
