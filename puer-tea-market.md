<p><p>The Pu'er tea market is primarily divided into two main types: raw tea and cooked tea. Raw Pu'er, or Sheng, is made from fresh leaves and undergoes minimal processing, allowing it to age and develop complex flavors over time. In contrast, cooked Pu'er, or Shou, is subjected to an accelerated fermentation process, resulting in a smoother and earthier taste. Both market types cater to different consumer preferences, with raw appealing to traditionalists and collectors, while cooked attracts those seeking immediate enjoyment.</p></p>
