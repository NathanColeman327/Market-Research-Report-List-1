<p><p>The pulmonary surfactant market is categorized into synthetic and animal-derived surfactants. Synthetic pulmonary surfactants, engineered in laboratories, offer consistency and tailored properties, enhancing their use in treating respiratory conditions like neonatal respiratory distress syndrome. In contrast, animal-derived surfactants are sourced from natural materials, primarily from bovine or porcine lungs, providing a complex mixture of lipids and proteins. Both types play crucial roles in improving lung function and reducing surface tension in the alveoli, aiding in better respiratory management.</p></p>
