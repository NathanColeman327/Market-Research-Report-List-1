<p><p>The textile dyestuff market consists of various types, each tailored for specific fibers and applications. Disperse dyes are primarily used for polyester fabrics, offering bright colors and excellent wash fastness. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fibers, making them ideal for cellulosic materials. Sulfur dyes are favored for their high resistance to fading and are commonly used on cotton. Vat dyes provide vibrant colors and stability, especially for cotton, while acid dyes are suitable for protein fibers like wool and silk, known for vivid shades.</p></p>
