<p><p>The textile fiber dyestuff market is categorized into several types based on their chemical composition and application processes. Disperse dyes are used for synthetic fibers, offering vibrant colors. Reactive dyes bond chemically with fibers, providing excellent wash-fastness. Sulfur dyes are cost-effective for dark shades, mainly on cotton. Vat dyes require a reduction process and offer lightfast properties. Acid dyes are used on protein fibers and nylons, providing bright colors. Other dyes encompass various specialized dyes catering to niche applications.</p></p>
