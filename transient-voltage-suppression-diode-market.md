<p><p>The transient-voltage-suppression (TVS) diode market is primarily segmented into uni-polar and bi-polar types. Uni-polar TVS diodes are designed to clamp positive voltage surges, making them suitable for protecting circuits against positive transients, while allowing negative voltages to pass. In contrast, bi-polar TVS diodes can handle both positive and negative voltage surges, offering comprehensive protection for alternating current applications. These distinctions make each type applicable for different environments, influencing their adoption in industries like telecommunications, automotive, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
